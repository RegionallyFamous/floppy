# Floppy Marketing

## Core Positioning

**Floppy is freedom for your files.**

It is a private file drive powered by WordPress, built for people who are tired of renting their digital life from SaaS companies.

Floppy gives you the familiar promise of sync, storage, and desktop access, but puts the control plane where it belongs: in infrastructure you own.

## One-Line Pitch

Floppy turns your WordPress site into a private Dropbox-like file drive that you own and control.

## Short Pitch

Floppy is a private file drive for WordPress. It gives you Dropbox-like storage and macOS sync without surrendering your files to another SaaS silo. Your WordPress site owns the storage, permissions, metadata, audit logs, and device tokens.

Own your data. Get rid of the SaaS. Let WordPress set your files free.

## The Big Idea

For years, files have been pushed into closed platforms. You upload, subscribe, sync, and hope the terms stay friendly.

Floppy points the other way.

Your website already has users, roles, permissions, media, storage, backups, admin screens, plugins, REST APIs, and a hosting account you control. Floppy turns that into a personal or team file drive.

It is not anti-cloud. It is pro-ownership.

## The Enemy

The enemy is not Dropbox specifically. The enemy is rented control:

- Paying forever for access to your own files
- Files scattered across closed SaaS accounts
- Export paths that feel like escape hatches
- Admins who cannot fully inspect, migrate, or automate their own storage
- Teams that want simple sync without handing their data model to another vendor

Floppy should never sound paranoid. The tone is confident, practical, and liberating.

## README Opening Copy

Floppy is freedom for your files.

It turns your WordPress site into a private Dropbox-like file drive, with storage, metadata, permissions, sync events, and device authorization living inside infrastructure you own.

This is not another SaaS silo. It is a practical path back to ownership: WordPress as the control plane, your hosting as the storage layer, and a native Mac app as the bridge to your desktop.

## Audience

### Primary

WordPress power users, agencies, indie builders, small teams, and open-web people who already trust WordPress and want file storage they control.

### Secondary

Privacy-conscious teams, self-hosting enthusiasts, plugin developers, and organizations that need a straightforward private file layer without buying another SaaS workspace.

### Not Yet

Large regulated enterprises, Windows-first teams, mobile-first users, or anyone who needs formal compliance guarantees in v1.

## Voice

Floppy should sound:

- Independent
- Practical
- Slightly rebellious
- Warm
- Technically credible
- Pro-WordPress
- Anti-lock-in, not anti-user

Avoid:

- Fearmongering
- Crypto-style sovereignty jargon
- Overpromising production readiness
- Pretending WordPress hosting is magic infrastructure
- Positioning as a total Dropbox replacement before sync is fully production-proven

## Product Proof Points

Use these when the message needs credibility:

- Custom indexed tables for file-system metadata
- Private blob storage with direct-access probes
- Authenticated file, preview, and thumbnail endpoints
- Scoped, revocable macOS device tokens
- WordPress Application Password onboarding
- GitHub-first plugin distribution
- Desktop Mode integration
- File Provider source target for Finder-native sync
- Audit logs and rate limits
- Export/repair tooling
- Load-test fixtures up to 100k and 1M metadata rows

## Launch Narrative

The old promise of the web was that you could own your place online.

Then files moved into SaaS silos. Documents here, photos there, client assets somewhere else, each one behind a new subscription and a new control panel.

Floppy brings files back into the open-web stack.

If WordPress can own your posts, pages, products, users, memberships, and media, it can own your private file drive too.

Floppy is the beginning of that idea: a private, WordPress-powered drive for people who want sync without surrender.
